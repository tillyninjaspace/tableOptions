ESL-181LS/ESL-981LS

Listening and Speaking, Level I (Low Intermediate)

This course is for students who already understand and speak basic English, that is, for high-beginning to low-intermediate English learners. In this course, students will practice listening and speaking skills, improve their fluency, and learn more vocabulary. To decide if this course is right for you, continue reading...

Do most of these statements describe you?

  • I can have short conversations about basic personal information, my family, my job, and my daily life.
  • I can communicate my needs and do simple tasks in English.
  • People often don’t understand what I say.
  • I sometimes don’t understand basic directions in English.
  • I can type and use a computer.

ESL-182LS/ESL-982LS

Listening and Speaking, Level II (Intermediate)

In this course, intermediate-level students will continue improving their listening, speaking, and communication skills in American English. The course focuses on fluency and vocabulary development, with some attention to verb tenses and sentence structure. To decide if this course is right for you, continue reading...

Do most of these statements describe you?

  • I can understand the main points when I listen to someone talk about familiar topics in standard English, but I miss some important details.
  • I can describe my experiences and ideas, but not yet on complex topics.  
  • I am still often afraid to talk with native speakers of English.
  • I can type and use a computer.

ESL-183LS/ESL-983LS

Listening and Speaking, Level III (High Intermediate)

This course is designed to help non-native speakers develop high-intermediate listening and speaking skills in American English, with an emphasis on fluency, comprehension, vocabulary development, verb tenses, beginning note-taking, and sentence structure. Not applicable to associate degree.

Do most of these statements describe you?

  • I can discuss my own experiences and familiar topics in English fairly easily.
  • I am beginning to understand class material outside of ESL classes.
  • I often watch TV/movies in English, but I don’t always understand everything.
  • I sometimes do not have the vocabulary I need to express my ideas and feelings in English.
  • I can type and use a computer.

ESL-184LS/ESL-984LS

Listening and Speaking, Level IV (Advanced)

This course is the highest level in a sequence of listening and speaking courses leading to the English proficiency needed for success in college-level courses. It helps students develop the communication skills they need for academic and professional purposes. It includes note-taking and responding to academic lectures and other presentations through group discussions, debates, oral presentations, and impromptu speeches. To decide if this course is right for you, continue reading...

Do most of these statements describe you?

  • I can interact with native speakers effectively in many (but not all) situations.  
  • I can understand some lectures on familiar topics, but I have trouble with advanced academic and professional language.
  • I sometimes struggle to effectively engage in complex discussions.
  • I still need to improve my note-taking skills.
